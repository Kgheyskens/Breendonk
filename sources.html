Sources

A curated list of academic and institutional sources consulted for this website.

The information presented throughout this website has been compiled from a combination of academic publications, institutional archives, memorial site materials, and survivor testimonies. The following bibliography is organized by source type and is intended to support further research and study.

Books and Monographs

  • Book Nefors, Patrick. Breendonk: Chronique d'un Camp (1940–1944). Brussels: Editions Racine, 2004.
  • Book Steinberg, Maxime. L'Étoile et le Fusil: La Question Juive 1940–1942. Brussels: Vie Ouvrière, 1983.
  • Book Steinberg, Maxime. The Holocaust and the Belgian Jews. Translated by the author. Jerusalem: Yad Vashem, 2004.
  • Book Adriaens, Ward. Breendonk: Getuigenissen uit het kamp. Antwerp: Manteau, 2006.
  • Book Schram, Laurence. Dossin: L'antichambre d'Auschwitz. Brussels: Editions Racine, 2017.
  • Book Conway, Martin. Collaboration in Belgium: Léon Degrelle and the Rexist Movement 1940–1944. New Haven: Yale University Press, 1993.
  • Book De Wever, Bruno. Greep naar de Macht: Vlaams-Nationalisme en Nieuwe Orde, het VNV 1933–1945. Tielt: Lannoo, 1994.
  • Book Wieviorka, Annette. The Era of the Witness. Translated by Jared Stark. Ithaca: Cornell University Press, 2006.

Institutional and Archival Sources

  • Institution Fort Breendonk National Memorial. Official exhibition materials and guided tour documentation. Willebroek, Belgium.
  • Archive CEGESOMA — Centre for Historical Research and Documentation on War and Contemporary Society. Belgian State Archives, Brussels.
  • Archive Kazerne Dossin — Memorial, Museum and Documentation Centre on Holocaust and Human Rights. Mechelen, Belgium.
  • Institution Yad Vashem — The World Holocaust Remembrance Center. Jerusalem, Israel. Online database and educational resources.
  • Institution United States Holocaust Memorial Museum (USHMM). Washington, D.C. Encyclopedia of Camps and Ghettos, Volume I.
  • Archive Belgian War Crimes Commission. Post-war investigation records and trial documentation, 1944–1951.

Testimonies and Oral Histories

  • Testimony Survivor testimonies collected by the Belgian War Crimes Commission, 1944–1949. Archived at the Belgian State Archives.
  • Testimony Visual History Archive, USC Shoah Foundation. Testimonies of Belgian Holocaust survivors, including former Breendonk prisoners.
  • Testimony Post-war trial proceedings and witness depositions from the prosecution of Breendonk camp personnel.

Academic Articles and Journals

  • Article Nefors, Patrick. "The Repression at Fort Breendonk: A Microhistory of Nazi Terror in Belgium." Journal of Belgian History 34, no. 2–3 (2004): 231–268.
  • Article Lagrou, Pieter. "The Politics of Memory: Resistance and Collaboration in Western Europe, 1945–1965." European Review of History 7, no. 1 (2000): 45–64.
  • Article Wouters, Nico. "Municipal Government during the German Occupation of Belgium, 1940–1944." Cahiers d'Histoire du Temps Présent 15 (2005): 109–134.
